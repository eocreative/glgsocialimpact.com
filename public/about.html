About Us

About GLG Social Impact

GLG helps top social sector leaders and organizations around the world accelerate their impact and scale by providing pro-bono and discounted memberships to GLG’s learning community. GLG Social Impact advances learning and decision-making among the social sector’s top innovators – from supporting grantmaking and mission-driven investing through industry analysis and market landscaping to helping nonprofits with technical, regulatory, and strategic challenges.

The GLG Social Impact Fellowship is our flagship program, providing learning resources to a select group of non-profits and social enterprise on a pro-bono basis.

GLG Social Impact also works with a range of other social sector clients including large nonprofits, social enterprises, foundations, and impact investing funds.

Case Studies

Examples of how GLG works with fellows and partners

CREA

CREA wanted to develop an online platform to both deliver its training virtually and to incorporate digital tools into the content. GLG Council Members provided insight into cost of different delivery models and providers, e-learning course development, and general understanding of the e-learning space. CREA secured a $significant grant from Google to roll out its e-learning course as part of a digital skills training that will reach over 10,000 women entrepreneurs across Mexico.

City Health Works

City Health Works needed to understand how to incorporate its in-home health services into the broader NYC healthcare system by accessing government programs like Medicaid and Medicare. By speaking with GLG Council Members from a non-profit health insurance company and the Director of Medical Economics at a large managed care organization, City Health Works learned about inclusions that will enable them to bill to these programs. City Health Works is now better position to engage with New York State regulations and reach more patients through the coming regulatory changes to Medicaid.

East Bali Cashews

In order to bring its cashews to the US Market via Amazon.com, East Bali Cashews needed to navigate the online retailer’s systems for setting up sales. East Bali Cashews’ CEO spoke to a former Director at Amazon.com who helped guide him through the process and made critical introductions at Amazon to fast track the launch of East Bali Cashews’ e-commerce store. A shipment of cashews is en route to the Amazon distribution center, and in June 2015, East Bali Cashews will be available for purchase on the site.

Nuru International

Nuru International’s expansion to Ethiopia included partnering with local agricultural cooperatives to commercialize produce. GLG recruited a former USAID professional who had worked extensively in Ethiopian Agricultural Cooperatives. The expert was able to connect Nuru’s team with professionals on the ground and demystify complex taxation issues related to Nuru’s project.

Global Village Fruit

Annie Ryu’s company, Global Village Fruit, brings jackfruit products from India to the global marketplace. Jackfruit is a meat substitute and the largest known tree-borne fruit. Annie came to GLG for expertise in packaging and product design and learned critical insight from the former VP of Marketing for Beyond Meat. Through this engagement, Annie learned how to position and sell her products in the US; Global Village Fruit will be hitting the shelves of Whole Foods in a few short months.

Spirit of America

Spirit of America provides targeted assistance to US troops and diplomats serving on humanitarian missions abroad. In Kurdistan, Spirit of America wanted to provide logistical support to Peshmerga forces by procuring and shipping equipment through local providers. In a consultation with a GLG Council Member who was a Lieutenant General with recent experience in Kurdistan, Spirit of America’s team learned that there were no local producers but that local hubs could facilitate the logistics of importing equipment.

BPeace

BPeace turned to GLG to find the right business executive to help Los Trillizos bakery in El Salvador improve its operations and products. A GLG Council Member with over 10 years’ experience in the bakery industry traveled to El Salvador to evaluate the operation and recommend improvements. During his week on the ground, the expert fine-tuned mixing processes to achieve larger output, and trained staff and chefs on the processes and also on the Good Manufacturing Practices methodology.

Partners

Since our company's founding 15 years ago, we have partnered with leading social sector organizations around the world. Below are some of the organizations which have leveraged GLG's network to enhance and accelerate impact. Contact us to to become a partner.




Spirit of America is a nonprofit organization dedicated to helping Americans serving abroad assist local people in need by providing materials and expertise on the ground. Please see a case study and op-ed about our partnership.

The Resolution Project, Inc. is dedicated to fostering youth leadership development around the world through collaborative social entrepreneurship.

Endeavor Global is a nonprofit that seeks to transform emerging economies by supporting high-impact entrepreneurs. Please see a blog post about our partnership.

The Bridgespan Group is a nonprofit advisor and resource for mission-driven organizations and philanthropists.

Echoing Green is a nonprofit that provides seed funding and technical assistance to emerging social entrepreneurs with ideas for social change

The Partnership Fund for New York City identifies and supports New York City’s most promising entrepreneurs in both the for-profit and not-for-profit sectors.

The Juilliard School is a performing arts conservatory which currently trains undergraduate and graduate students in dance, drama, and music.

LIFT’s mission is to help community members achieve economic stability and well-being. LIFT is working to establish a new standard for holistic and enduring solutions in our country’s fight against poverty.

Youth INC is a venture philanthropy organization that invests in nonprofits serving children in the New York City area. 

Khosla Impact is a venture fund that invests in market-based solutions and assists entrepreneurs developing products and services for people at the bottom of the world’s economic pyramid.

OneGoal identifies, trains and supports our nation's most effective teachers to lead underperforming high school students to reach their full potential and graduate from college.

The Wadhwani Foundation is a private grant-making foundation committed to improving standards of living in emerging economies through accelerating economic development.

Bpeace is a non-profit network of business professionals who volunteer skills to entrepreneurs in conflict-affected countries to help them create significant employment for all.

Achievement First is a high-performing network of 25 non-profit, college-preparatory, K-12 public charter schools in Connecticut, New York and Rhode Island.

Room to Read seeks to transform the lives of millions of children in Asia and Africa through education. We develop literacy skills and a habit of reading among primary school children and ensure girls have the skills and support needed to complete their secondary education.
