Zack Rosenberg

Co-Founder and Co-Director, St. Bernard Project

Sector: Disaster recovery

Zack Rosenburg is the co-founder and CEO of St. Bernard Project, a nationally renowned leader in disaster resilience and recovery. Zack directs the strategic vision, partnership development and operations of the organization. St. Bernard Project operates five offices nationwide, and has rebuilt more than 775 homes for families whose homes have been damaged in natural disasters.

Prior to launching SBP with his wife and co-founder, Liz McCartney, Zack ran a criminal defense practice in Washington, DC, where he received a Defender of Justice Award from the Mid-Atlantic Innocence Project in recognition of his work to free an man who spent more than 20 years of his life in jail for a murder that he did not commit. He received his J.D. from American University and an LLM from Georgetown University. Mr. Rosenburg was an E. Barrett Prettyman Fellow at the Georgetown University Law Centerâ€™s Criminal Justice Clinic.

Zack is a nationally recognized leader in post-disaster recovery and in socially-innovative approaches to solving complex, but solvable, social challenges. Zack and St. Bernard Project have won the Manhattan Institute Social Innovation Award, the New Orleaneans of the Year award and have been featured on the cover of US News and World Report, and in Newsweek, Wall Street Journal, and Washington Post. President Clinton and President Obama have both recognized SBP's work as an innovative solution to post-disaster recovery.
