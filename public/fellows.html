ABOUT GLG IMPACT FELLOWS

The Gerson Lehrman Group (GLG) is the world’s largest membership-based network for professional learning and expertise. With 20 offices globally and over 1,000 clients, GLG partners with organizations seeking targeted connections to insights by providing access to a global network of over 350,000 experts.

GLG Impact is an initiative that supports high-performing nonprofits and their leaders at critical growth junctures.
GLG Impact is part accelerator, part fellowship:

Accelerator

GLG Impact enables fellows and their senior management teams to accelerate the growth and impact of their organizations by leveraging our global network for unlimited, on-demand consultative services in partnership with GLG Research.

Fellowship

Leaders of high-impact nonprofits in the GLG community receive dynamic mentorship and counsel from business, policy, and academic experts to improve their leadership capacity and extend their networks.

WHO IS IN THE GLG NETWORK

Experts in the GLG network, called Council Members, join the GLG community to teach, to share and to learn. Council Members participate in GLG Impact to share their knowledge and skills with high-performing social sector leaders and to help solve global social problems within the areas of their expertise

  • 350,000 Council Members
  • Fortune 500 executives
  • Industry participants across the global economy
    • Technology, Energy, Education,
    • Telecom, Healthcare, Industrials
  • Academics, physicians, and research scientists
  • Policy, legal and regulatory Experts

WHO IS GLG IMPACT FOR

The strongest candidates for GLG Impact are passionate and tireless leaders of nonprofits with innovative solutions to create positive social change. Organizations considered must have between $750,000 - $10M in annual operating budget and focus aligned with one of the following:

  • Technology-enabled education
  • Professional development for underserved populations
  • Dissemination of expertise and knowledge
  • Social entrepreneurship
  • Leveraging networks for greater good
